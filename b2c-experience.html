The Underperforming Teammate

Scenario Context: You're working on a high-visibility project with tight deadlines. Your teammate Alex has missed three deadlines in the past month, and the team is starting to feel the pressure.

üé≠ The Situation

Setting: Coffee break in the office kitchen. Alex approaches you looking stressed.

Alex says: "Hey, I know I've been behind on some stuff lately. I'm really trying my best here, but everything feels overwhelming. I hope the team isn't too frustrated with me."

Your challenge: Address the performance issue while being supportive and maintaining your working relationship.

How would you respond?

A

Empathy First Approach

"I can see you're feeling stressed, Alex. That must be really tough. Let's sit down and figure out how we can support you better. What's been the biggest challenge for you lately?"

B

Direct but Kind

"Alex, I appreciate you acknowledging this. The missed deadlines have been affecting our project timeline. Can we talk about what specific support you need to get back on track?"

C

Solution-Focused

"Thanks for bringing this up, Alex. The team wants to see you succeed. What if we scheduled some time this week to break down your tasks and see where we can help streamline things?"

üí≠ AI Coaching Tips
Consider: How can you acknowledge their feelings while still addressing the impact? The best responses balance empathy with accountability.
